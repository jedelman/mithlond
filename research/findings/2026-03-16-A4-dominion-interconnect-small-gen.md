### What Was Found
Research into SCC Case PUR-2024-00211 reveals that for mid-sized generators (**250 kW to 20 MW**), the primary barrier is not the 20-year queue wait seen in the >100 MW bracket, but a **Direct Transfer Trip (DTT)** requirement. Effective February 2026, the SCC preliminarily approved a rule requiring DTT for projects >250 kW, adding **$500k to $1.5M** in infrastructure costs (fiber/relays) per project.

### Source
[SCC Docket PUR-2024-00211 (Interconnection Standards for DER)](https://scc.virginia.gov/); [Coalition for Community Solar Access (CCSA) Filing (Feb 2026)](https://communitysolaraccess.org/).

### Verification Status
**VERIFIED** 250 kW threshold and DTT cost estimates via SCC filings. **PARTIAL** on the exact wait-time comparison for Level 3 (up to 20 MW) generators vs the >100 MW cluster studies.

### Implications for Mithlond
A 500 kW–2 MW municipal compute pilot (Track B) utilizing onsite renewables would trigger the $1M+ DTT penalty. However, because the SNA Regional Connectivity Ring (RCR) provides municipal dark fiber, there is a strategic synergy to use the RCR to satisfy DTT requirements at a significantly lower cost than Dominion's estimated $1.5M.

### What Was Found
The Virginia DEQ has finalized a revision to **Guidance Memo APG-576**, establishing **"Tier 4-equivalent"** as the presumptive Best Available Control Technology (BACT) for data center diesel generators. This requirement applies to all air permit applications received on or after **July 1, 2026**. Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) and Diesel Particulate Filters (DPF) are now effectively mandated.

### Source
[Virginia Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) Guidance Memo APG-576 Revision (2026)](https://www.deq.virginia.gov/) and Virginia Regulatory Town Hall.

### Verification Status
**VERIFIED** via primary agency guidance and public comment records (March 9–April 8, 2026).

### Implications for Mithlond
This is a major victory for environmental accountability. It levels the playing field for municipal compute by increasing private data center cap-ex. It also provides a clear regulatory hook for the Hampton Roads region to demand higher standards for any new data center development.
